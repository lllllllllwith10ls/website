Tridrogen

Tridrogen is the third element on the hypoiodous table, and has three valence electron. Its symbol is T and is a gas that forms examples of overfilled bonds. It forms compounds with most nonmetallic elements. It is one of the N-drogens, which are common elements that terminate side chains.

Tridrogen is commonly found in hyperwater, most commonly as T2Dy, and sometimes as (T3)2Dy and T2Dy3. T+3, T2+2, and T3+ ions are released in triacids. It generally bonds with asymmetric 3-1 bonds, and can take electrons.
