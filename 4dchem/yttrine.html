Yttrine

Yttrine is the 19th element on the hypoiodous table, and has 15 valence electrons. Its symbol is Yn and is an element similar to yttum. At standard conditions yttrine is a solid that forms complex gray cluster molecules. In the gas phase, it takes the form of Yn2.

Yttrine is one of the elements commonly found grouped with yttum, and is thus a yttum group element. It is electron deficient compared to yttum, and can only form a maximum of 15 bonds compared to yttum's maximum of 16.

Yttum commonly forms lewis acids and becomes part of adducts like hexadecahyperfluoroyttrinate (YnHo16-), a weakly coordinating anion.

Yttum is also commonly found with a 28-electron shell in molecules, like tetrahyperfluoroyttrinic acid (MYnHo4)
